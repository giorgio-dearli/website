the problem

the people you care about most are quietly becoming names on a screen.

growing up, seeing your people was effortless. you were close by, your time was your own, and life hadn't piled on yet. adulthood changed all of that. you're busier, more drained, more scattered, and nothing stepped in to replace what you lost.

the surgeon general declared loneliness a public health epidemic with health impacts on par with smoking 15 cigarettes a day.

and social media has made it worse. you see your friends' lives through stories and feeds every day, creating an illusion of closeness that masks how disconnected you actually are. you feel like you're keeping up because you liked their photo, but you haven't sat across from them in months.

the caring was never the problem. the coordination was. there's nothing in your life designed to help you maintain your most important relationships. until now.
